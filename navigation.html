Public Transportation

St. Louis offers two main modes of public transportation: The MetroLink and the MetroBus.

The network is quite extensive between the rail-way and the street bus, but mastering the complete system will take some time.

Ride-Sharing

These services have allowed people in hard to reach places to be able to get around just as smoothly.

Ride sharing has allowed people to utilize their communities resources for transportation instead of conventional state-provided methods.

Companies such as Uber and Lyft have been competing with taxi companies by providing a competitive business model that is customer oriented.

We'll attach links below for you to check out their respective services and decide for yourself.

Last Mile Rides

More and more cities across the country are popping up with electric scooters and bikes for the public to use.

These not-so-new commodities are known as "last mile transportation" and they are supposed to make too long to walk, too short to drive treks a bit more manageable for the commuter.

If you need to get from one bus stop to another in a timely fashion you could benefit from checking these resources out when available.
